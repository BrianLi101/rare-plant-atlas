# Rare Plant Atlas

Rare Plant Atlas treats rare plants the way they deserve to be treated — as extraordinary living things with lineage, character, and depth.

We built this for collectors — people who already know their Philodendrons from their Anthuriums, who understand the difference between aurea and sectoral variegation, and who are evaluating whether a $200 cutting is worth buying. That person doesn't need a watering schedule. They need to understand how a plant actually grows, what variegation stability looks like over time, where the genetics came from, and what it's going to cost them to keep it alive long-term.

We built something cinematic. Each plant page is designed to give you a true feel for the plant: how it grows, what a healthy specimen looks like across leaf stages, how variegation expresses and stabilizes, how it propagates, and what it realistically costs to acquire and maintain. An experience that is detailed enough to be genuinely useful to anyone growing or considering a plant, and beautiful enough to do justice to the plants.

## Content notes

### Cinematic panels are visual-first
The `panels` content should focus on how the plant looks and feels in person. Prioritize visual qualities like leaf texture, variegation pattern, color contrast, shape, and flower presence. These sections are intentionally image-heavy, so copy should support what viewers are seeing (for example: dramatic foliage, beautiful leaves, striking blooms) rather than broad care instructions.
