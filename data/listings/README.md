# FAQ Guidance

FAQ sections on plant listing pages serve a dual purpose: they provide structured, scannable answers to the most common buyer questions (price, availability, value), and they significantly improve SEO and GEO performance by giving search engines and AI answer engines discrete, citable facts to surface directly in results. Since these FAQs are auto-generated from existing pricing data, they stay accurate as market conditions change without requiring manual upkeep.

Render all FAQs with `FAQPage` JSON-LD schema to enable Google FAQ rich results and direct citation by AI answer engines — the structured markup is as important as the content itself for GEO performance.

Below is guidance for auto-populating FAQs. Leverage existing data from the plant listing to populate its FAQs.

## General Guidance

- Monsteras are not worth it out of tissue culture unless you have an immense amount of patience. They usually grow quickly and cuttings spring up quickly but the growth rate is proportional to the thickness of the stem. Tissue culture monsteras take forever to grow so it is better to buy established plants given the risks of acclimating tissue culture and how fast monsteras propagate.
- Once rare plants get to a low range of $30-$50, they can decrease in price but won't go much further down.
- Plants available on Facebook Marketplace from local hobby growers tend to be 30% - 50% cheaper than the comparables from reputable online sellers who have additional operating costs for their businesses. These are riskier plants to buy if they are hard to identify, like any type of "mint" variegated monstera without a clear lineage. Plants that are hard to visually verify without established provenance are risky on Marketplace.
- Alocasias come down in price pretty quickly given they produce corms, even if they are not yet available in tissue culture. With variegated alocasias, it usually makes more sense to purchase a small plant instead of an unsprouted corm since variegation can be unstable. 

## Specific Questions


### Price & Value Questions (highest search intent)

- "How much does [plant] cost?" → answer with your price range by stage (tc through specimen), noting the low/high; also indicate the lowest the plant has cost compared to the current listing's lowest price

- "What is a fair price for [plant]?" → your market note + trend direction; use the lowest current listing price as a general indication of what the best available price could be

- "Why is [plant] so expensive?" → rarity tier + supply scarcity note; if the plant is not available in tissue culture, note that this increases price; if the plant has unstable variegation, note that this also increases price

### Buying Guidance Questions (high GEO value — AI assistants love these)

- "Where can I buy [plant]?" → your seller snapshot table (NSE Tropicals, Rare Plant Fairy, Etsy sellers, etc.)

- "Is [plant] available as tissue culture?" → your TC variant availability + price

- "What's the cheapest way to get [plant]?" → TC or cutting price vs. full grown comparison; if the plant's low-end price is under $100, note that it can likely also be found on Etsy or Facebook Marketplace

- "Is a tissue culture [plant] worth buying?" → TC price vs. cutting price + note on establishment timeline

### Rarity Questions

- "Is [plant] rare?" → your rarity tier field; include a note about whether it is available in tissue culture, as TC availability significantly affects long-term scarcity

- "Is [plant] getting easier to find?" → trend direction + TC availability; if TC exists, note that supply typically increases over time as labs scale production
